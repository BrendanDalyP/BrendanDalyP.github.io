Medallion Architecture

The Medallion Architecture is a data design pattern that organizes data into layers of increasing quality and refinement. This approach provides a clear, scalable framework for data processing and analytics workloads.

Key Benefits

Enables incremental data quality improvement, supports both batch and streaming workloads, and provides clear data lineage and governance capabilities.

Architecture Layers

  • Bronze Layer (Raw Data): Ingests raw data from various sources with minimal transformation
  • Silver Layer (Cleansed Data): Applies data quality rules, deduplication, and standardization
  • Gold Layer (Business Data): Creates aggregated, business-ready datasets for analytics

Data Vault Methodology

Data Vault 2.0 is a hybrid approach that combines the best of third normal form and dimensional modeling. It provides exceptional flexibility for handling complex, changing business requirements while maintaining data integrity and auditability.

Why Data Vault?

Ideal for organizations with complex, rapidly changing business requirements. Provides unparalleled flexibility and auditability for enterprise data warehousing.

Core Components

  • Hubs: Store unique business keys and metadata for core business entities
  • Links: Capture relationships between business entities over time
  • Satellites: Store descriptive attributes and context for hubs and links
